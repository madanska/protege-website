Venue

The short course will be held on the Stanford campus in the beautiful Li Ka Shing Center. The address of the venue is:

Li Ka Shing Learning and Knowledge Center (LKSC)
Room 205/206 (second floor)
291 Campus Drive
Stanford, CA 94305
View on Google Maps.

How to get there

The closest airports are the San Francisco International Airport (SFO) and the Mineta San Jose International Airport (SJC).

See below the main locations relevant to the course: Parking Structure 5 (if you are driving), and the different bus stops (if you are taking the Marguerite shuttle).

If you are driving

Detailed directions for driving to the Li Ka Shing Center are available here.

You will park in Parking Structure 5, and have a 5-7 minutes walk to the venue (see map below). We will provide daily visitor parking permits, which we will mail 2-weeks in advance of the event. You will have to park in the "Event Parking" which is only available on the top floor of the parking structure, and is marked by an "E" letter.

If you take public transportation and the free Marguerite shuttle

Stanford provides the free Marguerite shuttle to get around the campus. You will likely use the Santa Clara public transportation (VTA) to get to the Palo Alto Transit Center, and from there you may take several Marguerite shuttles that will get you to the course location (see below the detailed maps).

From the Palo Alto Transit Center, you may take Bus X to get you to the course location. Here is the schedule and map for Bus X. You need to get off at Roth Way @ Quarry Extension (at the Stanford hospital), and then walk to the course location.

Depending on where you stay, you may consider taking also other Marguerite shuttles to the course locations:

  • Bus C: schedule, map
  • Bus SLAC: schedule, map

Where to stay

There are many options for accommodation around the Stanford campus. Stanford has put together a lodging guide based on the proximity to the campus, which includes website links, rates and contact information. You may also take a look at the Palo Alto lodging guide.

You may find the best rates on the specialized travel sites, such as hotels.com, booking.com, and others.

You will be responsible for arranging your own accommodation.

Getting around

You may use the free Marguerite shuttle to get to the venue and to visit the campus. You may also use the Santa Clara public transportation (VTA) to get to the campus.

Visitor information

There are many fun and interesting things you can do on the Stanford campus. For example, you may take a self-guided or a 1-hour guided tour of the campus, get a panoramic view of the area at the Hoover Tower, visit the Cantor Arts Museum, and many more!
