Venue

The short course will be held on the Stanford campus in Redwood Hall building, room G19. The address of the venue is:

Redwood Hall
Room G19
243 Panama Street (in Jordan Quad)
Stanford, CA 94305
View on Google Maps.

How to get there

The closest airports are the San Francisco International Airport (SFO) and the Mineta San Jose International Airport (SJC).

If you are driving

If you are driving, please park in Parking Structure 5 (see on Google Maps) on the top floor in the section for Event Parking (marked with the letter E). Then, follow the walking instructions to the Redwood Hall building (see directions on Google Maps).

It takes about 8 - 10 minutes to get from the parking structure to the course venue, so please plan accordingly.

If you plan to drive, and you have checked this option in your registration, we will mail you the parking pass for the first day of the course. A parking pass is valid for one day. We will provide two more passes on the start of the course.

If you did not mark in your registration that you will drive, and you decided later that you will, please let us know asap, so that we can mail you the parking pass!

If you take public transportation and the free Marguerite shuttle

Stanford provides the free Marguerite shuttle to get around the campus. You will likely use the Santa Clara public transportation (VTA) to get to the Palo Alto Transit Center, and from there you may take several Marguerite shuttles that will get you to the course location.

Where to stay

There are many options for accommodation around the Stanford campus. Stanford has put together a lodging guide based on the proximity to the campus, which includes website links, rates and contact information. You may also take a look at the Palo Alto lodging guide.

You may find the best rates on the specialized travel sites, such as hotels.com, booking.com, and others.

You will be responsible for arranging your own accommodation and transportation.

Getting around

You may use the free Marguerite shuttle to get to the venue and to visit the campus. You may also use the Santa Clara public transportation (VTA) to get to the campus.

Visitor information

There are many fun and interesting things you can do on the Stanford campus. For example, you may take a self-guided or a 1-hour guided tour of the campus, get a panoramic view of the area at the Hoover Tower, visit the Cantor Arts Museum, and many more!
