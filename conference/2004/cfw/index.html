Call For Workshop Proposals

The Seventh International Protégé Conference will be held on July 6-9, 2004. The meeting will take place at the National Institutes of Health in Bethesda, Maryland. This meeting is traditionally a forum for users to meet one another and the Protege team and for us to meet you. We discuss common problems and solutions as well as current activities and plans. The meetings are fairly informal and also help the Protégé team to set priorities for development work. This will be the first meeting to take place in North America in over 8 years, following a long series of very successful meetings in Europe.

We invite proposals for workshops, to be held on the first day of the conference, July 6, 2004. All workshops will be half-day events. The purpose of the workshops is to provide an informal setting for participants to discuss technical issues and to exchange research ideas. We expect that most of the workshops will be interactive events, centering on discussions rather than presentations.

We strongly advise having more than one organizer, bringing different perspectives to the workshop topic. Workshop attendees must pay the conference registration fee.

Please note that due to a limited number of rooms at the conference venue, we will only be able to accept a very small number of workshops. We encourage submitters to contact us before the deadline if there is any concern about overlap with other similar workshop topics.

Proposal content:

The proposals to organize a workshop should be no more than 2 pages in length and should contain the following information:
  • The title and brief technical description of the workshop, specifying the goals and the technical issues that it will focus on.
  • A brief discussion of why the topic is of particular interest at this time.
  • A brief description of why and to whom the workshop is of interest.
  • A brief description of the proposed workshop format, discussing the mix of events such as paper presentations, panels, and general discussion.
  • The names and contact information (web page, email address) of the proposed organizers and short description of their relevant experience.
The workshop organizers will be responsible for:
  • Producing a "Call for Papers/Participation" for their workshop.
  • Arranging additional publicity for the workshop, such as distributing the Call to relevant newsgroups and mailing lists.
  • Producing a web page for the workshop and submitting the URL for the web page to the conference organizers no later than May 30, 2004.
  • Providing a brief description of the workshop for the conference web page.
  • Selecting the participants and the format of the workshop.
The Protege Conference Organizing Committee will be responsible for the following:
  • Providing publicity for the workshop series as a whole on the conference's web page.
  • Providing logistics support and a meeting place for the workshop.
