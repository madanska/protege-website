Call for Participation

Protégé OWL Workshop on Semantic Web and Description Logics

Rationale

Semantic Web technologies such as OWL and its foundation on Description Logics form a rapidly evolving field concerning not only the development and maintenance of ontologies (OWL, Prompt, CO-ODE), but also their application (reasoning, rule languages, ...). Protégé with the OWL Plugin has become one of the key tools in this area, and will help explore the potential and applicability of Semantic Web technology in real world projects.

Description

This workshop aims to:
  • presenting the latest developments in this area;
  • identifying the future needs;
  • discussing to what extend they can be integrated in the Protégé environment.
The workshop will consists of presentations and demos of the latest developments and the identification of future needs. Presentations are expected to last between 15 and 20 minutes. An open discussion in the end of the workshop will leave room for identifying future directions.

Topics

We invite presentations for the following topics (non restrictive list):
  • Relation of Description Logics with other formalisms such as frames
  • Extensions of OWL (metaclasses, rules...)
  • Best modeling practices and design patterns
  • Tools for editing and maintaining OWL ontologies
  • Reasoning (classification, rules...)
  • Semantic Web applications and Protégé
Notice: Due to space restrictions, we may need to limit the number of workshop attendees. Interested participants are invited to send us a statement of interest. If you want to give a presentation on one of the above topics, you should submit an extended abstract (around one or two pages), which will be published on the Workshop web site. Statement of interest for attendance and presentation submission should arrive before May 15th and should be sent to Holger Knublauch.

Intended public

This workshops is primarily directed to people already involved in OWL related projects, be it for tools or applications. Newcomers may rather want to attend the OWL tutorial session.

Organizers: Olivier Dameron, Matthew Horridge, and Holger Knublauch
