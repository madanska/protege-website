Call for Tutorial Proposals

The Seventh International Protégé Conference will be held on July 6-9, 2004. The meeting will take place at the National Institutes of Health in Bethesda, Maryland. This meeting is traditionally a forum for users to meet one another and the Protege team and for us to meet you. We discuss common problems and solutions as well as current activities and plans. The meetings are fairly informal and also help the Protégé team to set priorities for development work. This will be the first meeting to take place in North America in over 8 years, following a long series of very successful meetings in Europe.

We invite proposals for tutorials, to be held on the first day of the conference, July 6, 2004. Tutorials can be 90 minutes or 180 minutes in length.

Please note that due to a limited number of rooms at the conference venue, we will only be able to accept a very small number of tutorials. We encourage submitters to contact us before the deadline if there is any concern about overlap with other similar tutorial topics.

Proposal content:

The proposals to organize a tutorial should be no more than 2 pages in length and should contain the following information:
  • A brief description of the tutorial, including: goals, detailed outline, justification of why the tutorial is important, background knowledge required and potential attendees.
  • A brief description of why and to whom the tutorial is of interest.
  • A brief description of the proposed tutorial format.
  • Description of hands-on activities if any, plugins and other software software needed for running the hands-on activities, procedure to allow the participants to install the software on their computers as well as all the technical requirements needed for running it. The software needed for running the hands-on activities will be provided by the Tutorial speakers.
  • The names and contact information (web page, email address) of the proposed organizers and short description of their relevant experience.
The tutorial organizers will be responsible for:
  • Producing a web page for the tutorial and submitting the URL for the web page to the conference organizers no later than May 30, 2004.
  • Preparing materials for the tutorial for participants to download by June 20, 2004.
  • Providing a brief description of the tutorial for the conference web page.
The Protege Conference Organizing Committee will be responsible for the following:
  • Providing publicity for the tutorial series as a whole on the conference's web page.
  • Providing logistics support and a meeting place for the tutorial.
Important dates:

Submission deadline: March 1, 2004
Notification of workshop acceptance: March 14, 2004
URL for the tutorial page due: May 30, 2004
Tutorial materials due: June 20, 2004
Tutorial: July 6, 2004

Please send your submissions in PDF or ASCII to protege-conference-2004@smi.stanford.edu
