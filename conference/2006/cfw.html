Call for Workshops

We invite proposals for workshops, to be held on the first day of the conference, July 23, 2006. All workshops will be half-day events. The purpose of the workshops is to provide an informal setting for participants to discuss technical issues and to exchange research ideas. We expect that most of the workshops will be interactive events, centering on discussions rather than presentations. We strongly advise having more than one organizer, bringing different perspectives to the workshop topic. We also encourage submitters to contact us before the deadline if there is any concern about overlap with other similar workshop topics. Workshop attendees must pay the conference registration fee.

Proposal content:

The proposals to organize a workshop should be no more than 2 pages in length and should contain the following information:
  • A title and brief technical description of the workshop, specifying the goals and technical issues that it will focus on.
  • A brief discussion of why the topic is of particular interest at this time.
  • A brief description of why and to whom the workshop would be of interest.
  • A brief description of the proposed workshop format, discussing the mix of events such as paper presentations, panels, and general discussion.
  • The names and contact information (Web page, email address) of the proposed organizers and a short description of their relevant experience.

The workshop organizers will be responsible for:
  • Producing a "Call for Papers/Participation" for their workshop.
  • Arranging additional publicity for the workshop, such as distributing the Call to relevant newsgroups and mailing lists.
  • Producing a Web page for the workshop and submitting the URL of the Web page to the conference organizers no later than June 5, 2006.
  • Providing a brief description of the workshop for the conference Web page.
  • Selecting the participants and format of the workshop.

The Protégé Conference Organizing Committee will be responsible for the following:
  • Providing publicity for the workshop series as a whole on the conference's Web page.
  • Providing logistics support and a meeting place for the workshop.

Important dates:
Submission deadline: March 13, 2006
Notification of workshop acceptance: March 27, 2006
URL for the workshop page due: June 5, 2006
Workshop: July 23, 2006

Submission instructions:
Please send your submissions in PDF or ASCII to protege-conf-organizerslists.stanford.edu
